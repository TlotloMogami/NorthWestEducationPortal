North West Department of Education

Hon. MEC Viola Ntsetsao Motsumi.

The North West Department of Education is committed to improving access to quality education through innovation, transparency, and efficient service delivery. As part of this commitment, we are proud to introduce the Education Application and Placement Portal, a digital platform designed to simplify and modernize the school application process for parents and guardians across our province.

This platform also strengthens our ability to manage school capacity, improve planning, and enhance communication between schools, parents, and the Department. We encourage all parents and guardians to make use of this portal as we work together to build a more responsive and inclusive education system for the children of the North West Province.

How the process works

  1. Register & verify your details
  2. Submit learner application
  3. Upload required documents
  4. Pay the admission fee (R50)
  5. Track your application online

Required documents

  • Child’s report
  • Birth certificate
  • Proof of address
  • Medical certificate
  • Both parents’ certified ID

MEC Statement

Education innovation and transformation

The department derives its mandate from the: National Education Policy Act of 1996, which inscribes into law the policies, legislative and monitoring responsibilities of the Minister of Basic Education, and the formal relations between national and provincial authorities.

South African Schools Act of 1996, which promotes access to education, promotes quality and democratic governance in the schooling system, and makes schooling compulsory for children aged seven to 15 to ensure that all learners have access to quality education without discrimination. Employment of Educators Act of 1998, which regulates the professional, moral and ethical responsibilities of educators, and competency requirements for teachers.

Over the medium term, the department planned to focus on improving school infrastructure; providing support to improve matric completion rates; providing high‐quality support materials for learners and teachers; facilitating the increase in supply of quality teachers while preparing serving teachers to teach new subjects that will prepare learners for a changing world; improving services provided through the Early Childhood Development (ECD) function taken over from the social development sector; and providing nutritious meals for learners through the National School Nutrition Programme (NSNP).

Frequently Asked Questions

When do applications close?

Applications close on 20 March 2026.

How many schools can I apply to?

You can apply to up to five schools per learner.

How do I pay the R50 fee?

Pay online or request a reference pin for office payment.
